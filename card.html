King Cobra

The King cobra is the world's longest venomous snake. Despite the word cobra in it's name, king cobra's are not true cobra's. This is because the do not belong to the Naja genus but is the sole specie of the Ophiophagus genus.

  • Scientific Name: Ophiophagus genus.
  • Average Length: 5.85 meters.
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years.
  • Habitat: Tropical elevated basins.
  • Diet: Other snakes and lizards.
  • Defense Growling hiss: Non-aggresive, low pitched growl.

The king cobra's skin is olive green with black and white bands on the trunk that converge to the head. The king cobra is sexually dimorphic, with males being larger and paler in particular during the breeding season. The head of a mature snake can be quite massive and bulky in appearance. Like most extant snakes, due to macrostomy, it can expand its jaws to swallow large prey items.
